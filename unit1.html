Summary

Nelson Mandela was the first black president of South Africa. He spent 30 years in prison fighting against Apartheid (Racial Discrimination). He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993. He is known as an icon of peace because he forgave his enemies and united the country.

Important Dates
1963 Charged with capital offences (Rivonia Trial).
1993 Awarded Nobel Peace Prize.
1994 Became the first Black President.
2004 Formally left public life (Retirement).
Key Synonyms
  • Emancipation: Liberation, Freedom.
  • Resolve: Determination, Firmness.
  • Reconciliation: Settlement, Compromise.
  • Chasm: Gap, Division, Split.
