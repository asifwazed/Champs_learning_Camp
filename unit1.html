Part 2: The Struggle
Imprisoned for nearly three decades for his fight against white minority rule, Mandela never lost his resolve to fight for his people's emancipation. He was determined to bring down apartheid while avoiding a civil war.

Quick Summary

Mandela was South Africa's first black president. He fought against Apartheid (discrimination). He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993.

Important Dates
1963 Charged with capital offences.
1993 Awarded Nobel Peace Prize.
1994 Became First Black President.
2004 Left public life (Retired).
Flow Chart Tips

2. Spending 30 years in prison.
3. Determine to bring down apartheid.
