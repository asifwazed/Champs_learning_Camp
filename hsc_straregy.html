Exam Strategy

HOW TO SCORE SMART
Part A: Seen Comprehension (Shortcut)
  • The Keyword Trick: Weak students lose time translating. Don't read the passage first. Read the question, find the 2 main words (keywords), and scan the passage just to find those exact words. The answer is in that sentence.
  • MCQ Elimination: If you don't know the synonym, look at the 4 options. Usually, 2 options mean the exact same thing (so they are both wrong), 1 is completely opposite, and 1 is the answer.
  • "Why" Questions: If a short question asks "Why", find the keyword sentence in the passage and look for the words "because", "due to", or "in order to" right next to it.
Part B: Cloze & Rearrange (Survival)
  • Cloze WITHOUT Clues: If you are completely blank, guess these words: a, an, the, in, on, of, for, to. Over 60% of blanks in HSC are just basic articles and prepositions.
  • Cloze WITH Clues: You usually just have to add "ly" (adverb), "tion" (noun), or "ing" (verb) to the word in the box. Look at the word before the blank to guess which one.
  • Rearrange Chronology: Do not translate every line. Look for sentences starting with a Name/Title first (1), then birth year (2), then education/early life (3), their main achievement (4), and finally death/legacy (5).
Part C: Guided Writing (Format is King)
  • Paragraph Rule: Teachers check the format. A paragraph MUST be a single block of text. If you press 'Enter' and make two blocks, you lose 50% of the marks immediately. Keep it exactly 14-15 lines.
  • Story Writing: You MUST write a Title at the top and a Moral at the bottom. Even if your English inside the story is broken, a strong Title and Moral will guarantee passing marks.
  • Graph & Chart: Don't describe every number. Just write three things: What the graph is about (copy from the question), the Highest number, the Lowest number, and if the overall trend went Up or Down.
